Why MEMO exists

Evidence drift is a systems problem.

Medical device teams already maintain requirements, risk files, architecture notes, tests, reviews, and release records. The hard part is keeping their safety meaning synchronized after each software, configuration, interface, or behavior change.

01

Typed evidence model

Hazards, harms, controls, requirements, behavior, architecture, and verification evidence have explicit semantics.

02

Closure rules

Safety threads are checked for concrete allocation, behavioral anchoring, verification, and acceptance criteria.

03

Freshness under change

When linked assumptions, controls, flows, scenarios, or tests change, stale evidence becomes visible early.
