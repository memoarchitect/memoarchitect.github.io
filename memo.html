A | Evidence specification

MEMO defines the safety meaning behind the links.

The specification is the medical-device evidence model: typed entities, allowed relationships, methodology scope, closure constraints, and reference patterns that make safety threads explicit and reviewable.

github.com/memoarchitect/memo
Typed entities

What the model can talk about

Intended use, assumptions, hazards, harms, risk controls, requirements, architecture elements, behavioral flows, scenarios, verification items, and evidence results.

Relationship semantics

What the links mean

Risk controls mitigate hazards, requirements are satisfied by architecture, controls allocate to concrete locations, and verification evidence proves specific obligations.

Closure rules

What must be complete

A safety thread is not complete until the control is allocated, anchored to behavior, verified with acceptance criteria, and tied to a released baseline.
